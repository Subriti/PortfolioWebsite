Technology has become strongly intertwined to our daily lives. Technology can be seen nearly everywhere. From the tiniest chips to the greatest missiles, technology has contributed in the advancement of our existence. It makes our lives much easier and convenient; whether it's grocery shopping or purchasing research materials, we can do it all from the comfort of our own houses. In contrast to the days when letters took days to arrive, it allows us to connect with our loved ones in a matter of seconds. Additionally, IoT applications such as Amazon Echo and Google Home Mini act as personal assistants, doing errands, saving time and allowing us to live more efficiently.

The education sector has benefited greatly from technological advancements. Without needing to read pages and pages of a book, the solution to every question can be obtained in a fraction of a second. Smart learning has arisen as a concept that is accomplished by interactive video tutorials that not only entertain students but also convey all of the lesson's principles. It provides an engaging and pleasant environment.

Websites and apps for social media make it a lot easier for us to communicate with people. However, a true addiction to social media can lead to apathy and isolation in real life. Additionally, cyberbullying is a serious concern. Some people take advantage of their anonymity online, believing they can disguise their identities, to attack others with offensive words at their leisure. All of these things can be detrimental to our mental health and everyday well-being.

Also, the propagation of false news is aided by the availability of information over the Internet. It has the potential to deceive the public in order to achieve particular economic, social, or political objectives.
Privacy has also been a major concern. Everything we do online creates a trace that digital beasts may access at any moment. Our privacy may be further compromised with the growth of the Internet of Things as data is collected by our home assistants. Therefore, digital technology appears to be a double edged sword that must be used with caution

While technology has benefitted us, the threats it poses to our life should not be underestimated. Engineers must function in a more thoughtful and responsible manner as humans go into the future, and end-users must strike a balance between the use of technology systems and old-fashioned approaches for getting things done in a more healthy and productive manner.

Our society is, or will be, a digital society. The future is uncertain, and the opportunities are endless. So, what could be next? Is teleportation a possibility? With science, nothing is unlikely!
